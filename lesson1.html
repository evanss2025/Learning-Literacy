Using Web Browsers

Veda was assigned to create a research paper based on her favorite dog. She chose a poodle! However, she is not a poodle expert and will need to use outside sources to complete her paper.


She can use a search engine, a software program that helps people find the information they are looking for online using keywords or phrases. Common search engines include Google, Bing, Yahoo!, and Firefox.

However, it is important for Veda to use reliable resources, otherwise, her information may be incorrect.

To identify a reliable resource, first look at the author. They should have good credentials and knowledge in the field you are searching for. Then, look at the domain name. The domain name is the three letters that follow at the end of the website's domain name. The most credible sources are .gov and .edu. If the domain name ends in .com or .org be weary of the website.

Citing Websites

Since Veda is using this website for a research paper, she has to cite the website. This is because it is important to give credit to the original source when you are referring to it in your work.

To cite a website follow these steps,

1. Put the author’s first name and last name

2. Put the page title (a.k.a the paper’s name)

3. Put the website’s name

4. Put the month, day, and year the paper was created/updated

5. Lastly, put the URL of the website

Together it should look like, Author's last name, First name. “Page Title.” Website Name. Month Day, Year. URL.
