Online Security



Catherine recently got a new phone for her birthday! She is super excited to use it to text her friends and download social media, however, she firsts needs to learn and understand online safety.

There are some dangers online, such as cyberbullying, inappropriate content, and cyberharassment. Though this does not mean you will always encounter these dangers, it is important to know to make smart decisions online.



Where do these dangers occur?



Through a chat, there is a risk of cyberbullying or harassment. To combat this you can block the person harassing you, meaning you can stop them from contacting you. If a video game has a built-in chat or voice chat you can also block, report, or mute the harasser.

Be aware of spam emails in your email inbox. These spam emails can lead you to bad websites, give you false information, scamming, or even viruses.

When using social media don't share too much about yourself, you should never share your address or phone number. Also when using social media, think before you post. It is easy to not think how a comment or post could hurt someone.



Question: If Catherine encounters someone that keeps messaging her hateful comments, what should she do?



When creating accounts for apps or websites it is important to have a strong password. When creating a password, it is important to follow these rules.

The password should be at least ten characters (letters) long

It should be complex and unique

A combination of letters, numbers, and special characters

Should not resemble a name or common word

No personal information should be in the password
